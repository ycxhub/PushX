# Design: PushX MVP — Form Tracker with Session History

Generated by /ycx-office-hours on 2026-03-22
Branch: main | Status: DRAFT | Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement

People starting their fitness journey have no affordable, real-time feedback loop for exercise form. YouTube is passive and one-way — you watch, mimic, and hope. Human trainers are expensive ($50-150/session), inconsistent in quality, and don't personalize to individual body mechanics. Neither gives you a persistent record of how your form evolves over time.

The result: beginners plateau, can't tell if they're improving, and quit. The missing piece isn't motivation content (the market is drowning in that) — it's a **feedback loop that watches you, scores you, and shows you your progress.**

## Demand Evidence

- **Founder is the user.** Went from zero lifting to serious in 24 months using YouTube and trainers. Experienced the pain of no real-time feedback firsthand.
- **Existing market signal.** Simple tap-to-count pushup apps (no intelligence, no tracking) have thousands of App Store reviews and paying subscribers. Validates that people want to track pushups; the hypothesis is that intelligent tracking is a step-change improvement.
- **Conversations with beginners.** Founder spoke with people early in their gym journey who want low-cost, autonomous guidance. Not yet at the level of named users with specific pain — finding those 10 desperate users is the explicit goal of this MVP.
- **Demand risk (honest).** No waitlist, no one paying, no named user who'd scramble if PushX disappeared. This MVP exists to find (or fail to find) that person.

## Constraints

- **Pushups only.** No squats, bench, pull-ups, or other exercises. Nail one movement first. Expansion is a Series A story.
- **On-device only.** MediaPipe BlazePose (primary), Apple Vision (fallback). No cloud processing, no video uploads. Privacy is non-negotiable.
- **No backend.** No Supabase, no auth, no Sign in with Apple. All data is local (SwiftData). Backend comes after retention is proven.
- **Phone-on-floor setup friction is accepted.** This is a deliberate validation choice: if users endure the setup and still return, the core value is proven. Optimize onboarding later.
- **App Store submission.** Not TestFlight — the app ships publicly to eliminate distribution friction.
- **iOS only.** iPhone with front camera. No iPad, no Android, no web.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Bare Metal TestFlight" (Minimal Viable)

Polish Phase 0 into launch → camera → workout → summary. No persistence. Ship to TestFlight.

- Effort: S (human: ~1 week / AI-assisted: ~3 hours)
- Risk: Low
- Rejected because: Can't validate retention without history. A single session with no memory doesn't answer "do people come back?"

### Approach B: "Form Score + History" (Ideal Architecture)

Phase 0 + SwiftData persistence + session history + form trend + detailed per-session breakdown.

- Effort: M (human: ~3 weeks / AI-assisted: ~1 day)
- Risk: Medium
- Too heavy for first launch — polishing before validating.

### Approach C: "Scored Clip Share" (Distribution-First)

Phase 0 + auto-generated form-scored video clip + share sheet. No persistence.

- Effort: M (human: ~2 weeks / AI-assisted: ~1 day)
- Risk: Medium-High
- Rejected because: Sharing ≠ retention. Attracts spectators, not users. Doesn't answer the retention question.

## Recommended Approach: B-Lite — "Form Tracker with Lightweight History"

A trimmed version of Approach B. Enough history to show improvement across sets and days, but no elaborate analytics, no plans, no coaching.

### What ships

1. **Workout flow:** Launch → camera view → tap Start → real-time rep counting + form scoring overlay → tap Stop → session summary screen.
2. **Session summary:** Rep count, average form score, per-rep breakdown (depth, elbow angle, hip sag, consistency). Displayed immediately after the set.
3. **Session history (SwiftData, local only):** Each set saved as a session record. History view shows all sessions reverse-chronological with date, rep count, and form score.
4. **Form trend:** Simple visualization showing form score over the last N sessions. The user can see "am I getting better?" at a glance.
5. **Multi-set days:** A user might do 3 sets in a day and 2 sets the next day. Each set is its own session. The history and trend reflect all of them.

### What does NOT ship

- No backend / no Supabase
- No auth / no Sign in with Apple
- No training plans or workout programs
- No AI coach or voice feedback
- No social features or sharing
- No paywall (free for now — remove friction to adoption)
- No onboarding tutorial (deliberate: friction is the filter)
- No exercises other than pushups

### Data model (SwiftData, local)

- `PushupSession`: id, startedAt, endedAt, repCount, averageFormScore, reps (array)
- `PushupRep`: repNumber, depthScore, elbowScore, hipScore, overallScore, duration

## Open Questions

1. **What form score is "good enough" to ship?** The Phase 0 test protocol shows several failing tests (phantom reps, timing issues). How reliable does form scoring need to be for launch? Suggestion: it needs to be directionally correct — if someone does better pushups, the score should go up. Precision can improve post-launch.
2. **App Store review risk.** Camera-based apps sometimes get extra scrutiny. Need a clear privacy description and the app must function without sending any data off-device.
3. **How to find the first 20-30 users?** App Store organic discovery is near-zero for a new app. Founder needs a distribution plan: Reddit r/bodyweightfitness? Twitter/X fitness community? Friends? A "looking for beta testers" post?
4. **What does "3x/week for 4 weeks" look like in analytics?** With no backend, how do you measure retention? Options: manual check-ins with testers, optional anonymous analytics (TelemetryDeck or similar), or just ask people.
5. **Minimum iOS version?** MediaPipe + SwiftData both have minimum version requirements. Need to verify compatibility.

## Success Criteria

The MVP succeeds if, within 6 weeks of App Store launch:

- **10 users** use PushX **3+ times per week** for at least **3 consecutive weeks** without being prompted.
- **At least 3 users** report (via any channel) that they saw form improvement and that PushX helped them see it.
- **At least 1 user** asks for a feature (any feature) — evidence they want *more*, not just "it's fine."

The MVP fails if:

- Users try it once and never return (form feedback alone isn't a retention hook).
- Users can't get the camera setup to work and give up before completing a set.
- Form scores feel random or unhelpful — users don't trust the intelligence.

## Next Steps / The Assignment

1. **Fix Phase 0 reliability.** Before anything else: the rep counting and form scoring engines need to be directionally accurate. Phantom reps and failing test protocol items must be resolved. This is the foundation.
2. **Build B-Lite.** Workout flow → session summary → SwiftData persistence → history list → form trend. In that order.
3. **Prepare App Store submission.** App name, icon, screenshots, privacy policy (no data collected), description.
4. **Write a distribution plan.** Where do you find 30 people willing to try a pushup form tracker? Be specific — which subreddits, which communities, which friends.
5. **Ship and observe.** Don't add features. Watch what happens. Talk to every user who opens the app more than twice.
