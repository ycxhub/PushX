Glu AI Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 6, 2026

Medical and regulatory disclaimer: Glu AI is not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or a substitute for professional healthcare. Glu AI is not a medical device (including software-as-a-medical-device), not a continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) system or replacement for one, not a generic calorie tracker aimed at treating disease, and it does not prescribe, guarantee, or personalize medical outcomes. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional regarding glucose management, medications, insulin, allergies, metabolic conditions, and any dietary or lifestyle changes.

What Glu AI is: an educational meal-awareness companion that offers general educational information—based on broadly available knowledge about foods—about whether certain foods might be more or less associated with noticeable glucose responses for some people.

Glu AI does not diagnose conditions, prescribe or adjust therapies, interpret your personal glucose readings, or replace clinician judgment. Outputs are informational and educational only.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, process, share, and protect your information when you use the Glu AI mobile application, websites (including pages hosted on hard75.com), and related services (collectively, the “Services”). Capitalized terms used below have the meanings in Interpretation and definitions. By using the Services, you agree to this policy.

Information we may collect

Because Glu AI is a networked app with accounts and AI-assisted features, we may collect the following categories, depending on what you enable and submit:

  • Account and profile: identifiers needed to authenticate you (such as email address, user IDs from sign-in providers, or similar), security tokens, timezone or language preference, subscription status linked to your account.
  • User-provided meal and wellness content: text descriptions of meals, barcode or search queries for foods, photos you voluntarily upload while using the Services, timestamps, favorites, ratings, logs, feedback, support messages. Do not submit health information beyond what you are comfortable relying on solely for education.
  • Technical and usage signals: device type and operating system, app version, IP address, generalized location derived from IP, diagnostic logs relevant to outages and errors, timestamps of requests, aggregated feature usage counts if analytics are enabled.
  • Payments (if subscribed): transaction confirmations and receipts processed via third-party billing platforms—we do not store full payment-card numbers ourselves where card processing applies.
  • Legal and integrity: records needed to investigate abuse, comply with lawful requests when required, demonstrate consent, enforce our Terms, resolve disputes.

How we use information

  • Operate, personalize, troubleshoot, sync, backup, maintain security, authenticate users, fulfill subscriptions.
  • Run AI-assisted text and image pipelines to produce educational, non-prescriptive insights about foods and glucose awareness—not individualized medical judgments.
  • Improve reliability and models through aggregate, de-identified, or anonymized learning where permitted by law and this policy.
  • Communicate transactional notices such as resets, confirmations, outages, substantive policy notices, and optional marketing where you have agreed.
  • Comply with law, cooperate with lawful requests served on us.

User content and improvement of models

By submitting user content, you grant us a license to host, process, and use it to operate and improve the Services, including internal quality and model improvement, consistent with our commitments in this policy and in-product settings. We do not sell your personal data.
